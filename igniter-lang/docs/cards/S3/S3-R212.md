# S3-R212 - Source-Backed Level 2 Vocabulary/Spec Boundary

Status: planned
Date: 2026-05-30

Dispatch: R212 = C1-D -> C2-P1 -> C3-X -> C4-A -> C5-S

Pattern:
- C1-D designs the internal vocabulary/spec boundary for accepted source-backed
  Level 2 proof-local evidence.
- C2-P1 surveys safe documentation/status/spec target surfaces.
- C3-X pressure-checks wording, claim boundaries, and closed surfaces.
- C4-A accepts, conditions, holds, or redirects and chooses the next route.
- C5-S curates the result and exact next dispatch.

Round intent:
- Make the R211 achievement understandable without overstating it.
- Define what may be named in internal docs/status after source-backed Level 2
  proof acceptance.
- Keep source-backed Level 2 evidence non-canonical, proof-local, and
  no-authority.
- Prevent drift from "source-backed proof-local evidence" into public runtime,
  report/API, cache/dependency, production, Spark, or live non-selected branch
  evaluation claims.

Governing principle:

```text
Runtime is lazy.
Audit is aware.
Dry-run, if ever accepted, must be isolated.
Evidence must be sourced before it can be explained.
Vocabulary must not outrun authority.
```

Current accepted state:

```text

  SB-1..SB-15 / 61/61 PASS.
  C3-X pressure 15/15 PASS.

  Source artifacts are proof-owned SemanticIR-shaped JSON.
  Refs use sha256:<hex>.
  Live runtime remains lazy.
  report/result/receipt/cache/API/Spark/public authority remains closed.

Next allowed route:
  Design-only vocabulary/spec boundary.
```
